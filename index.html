How AIPAC Keeps New York Congress in Line with Top-Dollar Spending

More than half of New York's Congressional Delegation has received tens of thousands of dollars from the largest pro-Israel PAC in America.

DECEMBER 15, 2023 · By Kimberly Izar


The largest pro-Israel PAC in the country – American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC) – has contributed over $1 million to New York’s sitting congress members since 2022. There are no U.S. representatives who have accepted contributions from AIPAC that have publicly called for a ceasefire.

Of the 26 sitting representatives, only three of them have called for an immediate ceasefire in Gaza – Representatives Nydia Velázquez, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, and Jamaal Bowman. Other members of congress have introduced H. Res. 786, the Ceasefire Now Resolution, urging the Biden Administration to call for a ceasefire in Palestine and Israel and to allow humanitarian aid into Gaza.


Thousands of protestors have hit the streets to demand a ceasefire as the death toll in Palestine topples over 17,000 people since October 7, according to the Gaza Health Ministry.

Biz Peterson was one of those attendees at a pro-Palestinian rally last month. She said she’s been calling her representatives everyday.

“A ceasefire is only the start,” she told The Indypendent. “I’m just hoping that politicians are going to start learning the lesson that come 2028, Gen Z and millennials are going to be the majority in this country. We will hold the voting power and we will vote them out of office.”
